When shopping for a diamond, consider its cut, color, clarity, carat weight, and shape.

Cut
Excellent
Reflects nearly all of the light that enters the diamond, achieving the most brilliance.

Very Good
Reflects most of the light that enters the diamond. Achieves similar brilliance to excellent cut under normal lighting conditions.

Good
Reflects a large amount of light that enters the diamond, achieving brilliance at a more attractive value.

Fair
Allows a large amount of the light entering the diamond to escape, resulting in less brilliance.

Poor
Allows most of the light entering the diamond to escape, resulting in a dull or glassy appearance.

Color
D-F
Small traces of color can be detected by a gemologist, but not to the untrained eye. Colorless diamonds are very rare.

G-J
Appears colorless, but slight color is noticeable when compared to higher color grades. Near colorless diamonds provide excellent value.

K-M
Faint color can be detected by the naked eye. When set against a darker color (such as yellow gold), the color of the diamond is less noticeable.

Clarity
FL, IF
Inclusions are not visible under 10x magnification. These diamonds have very slight blemishes, if any, and are extremely rare.

VVS1, VVS2
Inclusions are difficult to see under 10x magnification. VVS1 inclusions are located in the bottom-half (pavilion), and VVS2 inclusions are located in the top-half (crown).

VS1, VS2
Inclusions are slightly visible under 10x magnification. VS1 diamonds contain fewer inclusions than VS2 diamonds.

SI1, SI2
Inclusions are noticeable under 10x magnification, but usually cannot be seen by the naked eye.

I1, I2, I3
Inclusions are obvious under 10x magnification, and can usually be seen by the naked eye.

Carat Weight
A diamond's carat weight refers to its weight, not size. The cut quality will impact how large the diamond appears — higher cut grades will make a diamond appear larger.
